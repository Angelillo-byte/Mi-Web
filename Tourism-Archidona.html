Information about Archidona

Archidona is a town in Andalusia with a rich history dating back to prehistoric times. It has witnessed the passage of various civilizations, from the Iberians and Romans to the Visigoths and Muslims.

During the Muslim period, Archidona gained significant importance and was one of the main defensive strongholds in the region, highlighted by its emblematic castle and fortress that still dominates the landscape. In 1462, it was conquered by the Catholic Monarchs, becoming part of the Kingdom of Castile.

Today, Archidona is known for its historic center with Baroque style, its famous Plaza Ochavada, and its vibrant cultural traditions reflected in its festivities, gastronomy, and local crafts. It is a destination that combines history, culture, and nature, offering an authentic experience of rural Andalusia.

Archidona is a charming Andalusian village located in the province of Malaga, known for its rich history, architecture, and traditions.

With cobbled streets and traditional Andalusian houses, Archidona retains an authentic and picturesque atmosphere that invites you to explore its corners. One of its most emblematic places is the Plaza Ochavada, a jewel of Andalusian Baroque that has become a symbol of the town.

In addition to its historical heritage, Archidona offers delicious gastronomy, with typical products such as olive oil, cured meats, and artisanal sweets. Surrounded by mountains and nature, it is an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility, culture, and unique landscapes in the heart of Andalusia.
